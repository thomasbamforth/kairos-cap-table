Key terms

▾
SAFE
Simple Agreement for Future Equity — an investment contract that converts to equity at a later priced round, not a loan.
Valuation Cap
The maximum company valuation at which a SAFE converts, giving the investor a ceiling on dilution and a floor on ownership.
Discount Rate
A percentage discount off the Series A share price that SAFE holders receive as compensation for early risk.
Option Pool
Shares reserved for future employee grants. Typically 10–20% at Series A and often expanded ("topped up") before the round closes, diluting existing holders.
Pre-Money Valuation
Company value immediately before a new investment closes. Post-money = pre-money + amount raised.
Conversion Event
A qualified financing (typically the first priced round) that triggers SAFEs to convert into preferred shares.
Phantom Dilution
The gap between a founder's cap-table-on-paper and real ownership once outstanding SAFEs convert. Easy to overlook because no shares have been issued yet.
Post-Money SAFE
YC's 2018+ SAFE variant where ownership is locked as a % of post-money valuation. Simpler math, but all dilution from subsequent SAFEs falls on founders.
Pro-Rata Rights
The right for an existing investor to participate in future rounds to maintain their ownership percentage.
